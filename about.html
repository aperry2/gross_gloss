THE GROSS GLOSS

About
~~~~~
This digital CD-ROM publication of the Gross Gloss is the first of its kind. We hope you enjoy this multimedia experience of timeless and transcendent literature. The intuitive computer interface, exemplary supplemental information on its creation, and innovative multimedia viewer allows anyone to explore the entire text, accessible in its entirety for the first time. It’s like having three libraries at home – always available to give you the answers.

Provenance ////
This digital CD-ROM publication was assembled from multiple sources, including the manuscripts and early printed books present in the Newberry Library, Bodleian Library, and Morgan Library. All primary sources have been successfully dated to the 14th Century. Secondary sources date to either the 17th or 20th Centuries. Images were collected from ephemera, research notes, and other secondary sources and may not fully represent the actual images stored as data in the book. Numerological analysis has confirmed that the authorship of this brand-new digital CD-ROM edition is only 9% machine-written, with the other 91% demonstrating human-written stochastic patterns.

Context ////
This digital CD-ROM edition preserves the feel and touch of the original manuscript versions while adapting their visual and symbolic systems for use with a modern computer with a windowed operating system. Every effort has been made to maintain fidelity to the 14th Century source material while presenting a stable and readable interface. The only artistic licenses exercised were in order to format the original versions to best fit your computing environment.

What is a Volvelle? ////
A volvelle, or movable paper disk, is an early modern book technology that allows readers, or users, to encode and/or reference information. The volvelles present in this CD-ROM edition were recovered from the only extant copy of an early print edition. They have been only slightly modified or digitally restored where necessary.
